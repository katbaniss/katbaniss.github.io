Mobile phone users in developing countries

Mode of access

Mobile technology has become part of everyday life and is also a major driver for social and economic development in developing countries. Due to the dominating poverty in these countries, people buy inexpensive mobile phones and access the internet through web browsers to use social networking sites to stay in touch with their family and friends or for mobile payment transactions.
Developing countries have many rural areas where certain capabilities must exist in order for inhabitants to access the mobile web. For businesses, it is critical to think about the different capabilities consumers have in those countries: (in addition to the outlined player in the section accessibility):


  1. Phone interface
    • Limited literacy requires special graphical and voice features.
  2. Power sources due to lack of infrastructure
    • Cell phone battery charger powered by bicycles.
    • Motorcycles and heat from fire.
  3. Websites with mobile payment options
    • Lack of bank account ownership requires option to pay online
    • Peer- to peer transaction: (Globe Telecom’s G-Cash and Smart Communications’ Smart Money, ProMobile permits inexpensive transfers of money to others).

Source: Wearesocial, 2014
